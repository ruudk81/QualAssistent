## Impact of Branding: Key Findings

### Credibility Effect

**Brand Adds Legitimacy** (line 677-678)
- Not Menton specifically, but having ANY brand makes it more credible
- Unbranded looked "work in progress" (from Topic 5)
- Brand signals it's a real product, not a concept

**Color Matters More Than Logo** (lines 665-666, 679-680)
- Blue = freshness association
- Color scheme does heavy lifting for communicating product benefits
- Brand name less important than visual design

### Menton Brand Perceptions

**Suitable/Fine/Credible** (lines 733, 736)
- Repeated use of lukewarm descriptors: "fine," "suitable," "credible"
- No strong enthusiasm for Menton specifically
- But no rejection either - acceptable carrier brand

**Similar to Market Offerings** (line 727)
- "Pretty similar to other things on the market"
- Menton with these concepts = familiar, expected, safe
- Not differentiated but not problematic

### Brand Doesn't Override Functional Concerns

**Still Worried About Dispenser** (lines 682-692)
- Brand doesn't make durability concerns go away
- Still need to actually try mechanism

**Contents Still Most Important** (line 728)

### Package-Concept-Brand Alignment

**Long Extreme Fresh + Tab Package + Menton = Best Fit** (lines 743-744)

## Shift from Unbranded to Branded

### What Changed
- Went from "work in progress" feel to "real product" (lines 508-512 vs. 677-678)
- Added legitimacy through branding presence
- Color became more obviously intentional (communicating freshness)

### What Didn't Change
- Functional concerns about durability, noise, reliability
- Preference for smaller tab only
- Need to try mechanisms before committing
- Content prioritized over package

### Menton Brand Role
- **Enabler not hero**: Makes concepts acceptable/credible but doesn't drive excitement
- **Safety**: Familiar brand reduces risk of trying new formats
- **Expected**: Menton in this category makes sense, fits naturally
- **Not differentiating**: Similar to competitors, doesn't stand out
