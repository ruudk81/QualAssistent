## Overview
This section explored participants' current usage of breath freshening products, including what they use, when/why they use them, which brands they prefer, and what frustrates them about existing products.

## Products Currently Used

**Primary Products: Pastilles & Chewing Gum**
- "Pastilles or kauwgom for me" (line 58)
- Universal agreement on pastilles as primary product
- Gum mentioned as alternative option

**Why Pastilles Over Other Products**

*Mouthwash* (lines 110-117)
- Used more at home, not portable: "I would not take mouthwash in the car" (line 111)
- Too strong: "it's a little bit too strong... it stays in my mouth and it's not so good" (lines 115-116)
- Contains alcohol: "not very smart to use them, not while you're driving" (line 117)

*Sprays* (line 113)
- "Sound a little bit disgusting, like something American"
- Not preferred

**Preference for Pastilles vs. Gum** (lines 148-156)
- Pastilles digestible, gum stays in digestive tract longer (lines 148-151)
- Gum disposal problem: "you need to spit it out or get rid of it in the trash" (line 153)
- Xylitol gum causes laxative effects and gassiness (line 148)
- Both can cause laxative effects if whole package consumed (lines 149-150)

## Key Insights

1. **Multi-functional product**: Pastilles serve multiple purposes beyond breath freshening - dental health, dry mouth, sore throat, focus/calming, entertainment
2. **Psychological > Actual effect**: Confidence and feeling fresh matters more than whether breath actually improves
3. **Xylitol = dental health**: Strong association between xylitol and dental care benefits
4. **Packaging is critical**: Spillage in bags is major frustration
5. **Social product**: Often shared with colleagues/friends, used openly
6. **Strength spectrum**: Need for both strong (breath freshening) and milder (post-meal) options
7. **Home vs. portable**: Different product choices for home (mouthwash) vs. on-the-go (pastilles)
